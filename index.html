Key Priorities for Gosforth

The issues that matter most to our community

🏣

A Thriving High Street

Gosforth High Street is the heart of our community. I'll champion our independent shops, cafes, and businesses — fighting for a High Street that's vibrant, accessible, and built around what residents actually want.

🚌

Transport That Works for Everyone

Better bus services, safer streets for walking and cycling, and traffic management shaped by genuine consultation — not top-down decisions. Gosforth residents deserve a say in how our streets are designed.

🌱

Protecting Our Green Spaces

From Gosforth Central Park to our tree-lined streets, I'll defend the green spaces that make our ward special — resisting overdevelopment and investing in play areas, community gardens, and cleaner air for families.

🏘

Homes People Can Afford

Rising prices are pushing young families and key workers out of Gosforth. I'll push for genuinely affordable housing in new developments and stand against schemes that don't serve our community's needs.

🏫

Excellent Schools & Opportunities

Gosforth's schools are among the best in the region. I'll fight to protect school funding, expand opportunities for young people, and ensure every child in our ward gets the start they deserve.

🤝

Health & Community Wellbeing

As a trustee of Citizens Advice and HealthWORKS, I've seen first-hand how the cost-of-living crisis and NHS pressures hit families. I'll be a councillor who fights for better GP access, mental health support, and community services.

About Jonathan

I'm Jonathan Sabarre — a father, a community volunteer, and someone who believes Gosforth deserves a councillor with the energy and experience to make a real difference.

By day, I'm Director of Marketing & Communications at Newcastle University, one of the city's largest employers. I hold a PhD in Health Informatics and I'm a Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Marketing, with over 20 years of experience leading teams, solving problems, and delivering results across the public, private, and higher education sectors.

But my commitment to public service goes well beyond my career. I serve as a Parish Councillor in Woolsington, I'm a trustee of Citizens Advice Gateshead — where I see every day how policy decisions affect real people — and I'm a trustee of HealthWORKS Newcastle, working to improve health outcomes in our communities.

"I've spent my career bringing people together, listening to what matters, and getting things done. That's exactly what I'll do for Gosforth."

I'm standing for Labour because I believe in a council that puts residents first — one that invests in our High Street, protects our green spaces, supports our schools, and ensures every voice in Gosforth is heard.
